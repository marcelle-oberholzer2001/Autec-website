Overview

Containerized gold recovery, engineered for the field.

The SA Autec Mobile Containerised CIL Plant is purpose-built for remote gold mining operations where rapid deployment, operational efficiency, security and high recovery are critical. It draws on decades of practical gold processing experience, pairing proven technology with modern engineering for a highly efficient, cost-effective recovery solution.

The entire plant is housed within standard shipping containers — one dedicated milling module and four integrated CIL agitation modules — enabling rapid mobilisation, simplified transport and fast site installation.

At a Glance

  • 10-ton feed bin, 55kW stirred pin mill regrind circuit, and dual centrifugal concentrators for immediate gold capture
  • Operates on less than 150kVA — smaller generators, lower fuel costs, easier remote deployment
  • Automated carbon and concentrate handling, with locked, fully enclosed compartments for security
  • Field-proven, including plant commissioning at a gold operation in Bindura, Zimbabwe
  • Strong residual value — relocatable, refurbishable and resaleable at the end of a project

Modular Containerised Design

The entire plant is housed within standard shipping containers: one dedicated milling module and four integrated CIL agitation modules. This modular format enables rapid mobilisation, simplified transport, and fast site installation, cutting project implementation timelines and infrastructure requirements.

  • Rapid mobilisation and transport
  • Fast site installation
  • Reduced infrastructure requirements
  • Shorter project implementation timelines

Integrated High-Efficiency Milling Circuit

The milling module combines feed handling, conditioning, screening and regrind into one integrated process, engineered to work together for maximum throughput.

  • 10-ton feed bin with automated screw conveyor feed
  • 2,500-litre slurry conditioning and de-clumping tank
  • Trash removal screening system
  • 55kW stirred pin mill regrind circuit
  • Dual centrifugal concentrators
  • Integrated slurry transfer pumps

Engineered for Reliability

Every transfer point, chute, launder and process connection is professionally designed using advanced CAD engineering software to optimise material flow.

  • Minimal spillages
  • Reduced blockages
  • Lower maintenance requirements
  • Reduced operator intervention
  • Increased plant availability
  • Higher overall production efficiency

Variable Speed Process Control

The feed conveyor, regrind mill and slurry pumps are all controlled by Variable Speed Drives (VSDs), letting operators fine-tune process parameters in real time.

  • Optimised plant performance
  • Improved recovery rates
  • Reduced energy consumption
  • Greater operational flexibility
  • Enhanced process stability

Energy-Efficient Regrind Technology

The plant uses a high-performance stirred pin mill running on ceramic grinding media — a significant step up from conventional ball mills.

  • More than 40% lower energy consumption
  • Superior grinding efficiency
  • Improved liberation of gold particles
  • Faster maintenance procedures
  • Reduced downtime

Advanced Ceramic Grinding Media

Ceramic grinding media delivers significant operational advantages over traditional steel grinding balls.

  • Up to 50% reduction in grinding media costs
  • Extended media lifespan
  • Reduced maintenance requirements
  • Elimination of cyanide consumption caused by steel media
  • Improved leaching efficiency and gold recovery

Gravity Concentration for Gold Recovery

Dual centrifugal concentrators are built directly into the milling circuit to recover free gold immediately after liberation.

  • Increased overall gold recovery
  • Immediate capture of coarse free gold
  • Improved project cash flow
  • Reduced gold losses to tailings
  • Faster return on investment

Automated Concentrate Handling & Security

The gravity concentrators flush automatically, depositing concentrate into secure storage within the milling container.

  • Eliminates manual cleaning
  • Enhances operational security
  • Reduces labour requirements
  • Minimises gold theft risk
  • Simplifies concentrate management

Low-Power, High-Efficiency Agitation

Specially designed high-efficiency impellers provide excellent mixing performance at exceptionally low power draw — each CIL compartment requires only 1.5kW while maintaining effective suspension and carbon contact.

  • Lower operating costs
  • Reduced generator requirements
  • Improved leaching performance
  • Lower carbon footprint

Simplified Maintenance Philosophy

Mixer drive systems use standard bearings, belts, pulleys and mechanical components available worldwide.

  • Rapid maintenance turnaround
  • Reduced spare parts inventory
  • Lower maintenance costs
  • Increased plant uptime

Industry-Leading Carbon Security

Security is built into every aspect of the plant design.

  • Locked access points
  • Fully enclosed carbon compartments
  • Automated carbon transfer systems
  • Secure concentrate storage
  • Minimal manual handling

Automated Carbon Management

Carbon movement between compartments is handled automatically by dedicated carbon transfer pumps.

  • Improved operational efficiency
  • Consistent carbon movement
  • Reduced labour requirements
  • Enhanced plant security
  • Improved gold adsorption performance

Automated Carbon Cleaning & Recovery

Loaded carbon is automatically transferred to a dedicated carbon handling container, where it is screened, cleaned and discharged into one-ton bulk bags ready for transport to the elution plant.

  • Elimination of manual carbon handling
  • Improved safety
  • Reduced contamination
  • Enhanced security
  • Streamlined logistics

Optimised CIL Flow Design

Short distances between compartments and strategically positioned carbon retention screens are engineered for reliable pulp flow, preventing common CIL operating problems.

  • Fewer tank overflows
  • Reduced carbon losses
  • Fewer blockages
  • Less downtime
  • Fewer process inefficiencies

Low Overall Power Requirement

The complete plant operates on less than 150kVA of electrical power.

  • Smaller generator installations
  • Reduced fuel consumption
  • Lower operating costs
  • Easier deployment in remote locations
  • Improved project economics
